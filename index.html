How it works

Three clicks from video to clean text

TranscriptSnap reads the transcript YouTube already renders on the page. That's why it needs no API, no key and no connection.

STEP 01

Open a video

On any youtube.com/watch page, click the TranscriptSnap pill in the corner or the toolbar icon.

STEP 02

Hit Transcribe

The transcript is read from the page, de-duplicated, stripped of timestamps and sound cues, and regrouped into paragraphs.

STEP 03

Copy or download

Take it to your clipboard or save a .txt file. Want the timestamps back? One toggle brings them in.

What you get

Small tool, no surprises

Actually readable output

Caption fragments get merged into sentences and paragraphs at natural pauses, instead of dumping 400 two-second lines.

Timestamps on demand

Off by default for reading and pasting. Flip them on when you need to cite a moment or jump back to it.

Clear status, every step

A chip tells you exactly where you are: Ready, Transcribing, Transcribed, Copied — or a plain-English reason it couldn't.

Four permissions, no more

activeTab, scripting, storage and youtube.com. Storage keeps one setting: timestamps on or off.

Privacy

The transcript never leaves your browser

This isn't a policy promise wrapped around a data pipeline. There is no pipeline. The extension makes no network requests of any kind — you can verify it in the DevTools network tab, or read the source, which is short.

What is collected

Nothing. No transcript text, no video IDs, no usage events, no identifiers.

What is stored

One boolean in Chrome's local storage: whether you want timestamps shown. It's removed when you uninstall.

FAQ

Questions people actually ask

Does TranscriptSnap send my transcripts anywhere?

No. The extension makes no network requests at all. It reads the transcript YouTube already renders on the page, cleans it in memory, and shows it to you. Nothing is uploaded, logged or stored.

Which videos work?

Any YouTube video that has captions, including auto-generated ones. If YouTube offers a "Show transcript" button for the video, TranscriptSnap can clean it. If the uploader disabled captions, there is no transcript to extract and you'll see a clear message saying so.

Do I need an account or an API key?

No. There is no sign-up, no key and no paid tier. Install it and it works.

What permissions does it ask for?

activeTab, scripting and storage, plus host access to youtube.com. Storage holds a single preference — whether timestamps are shown. No transcript text is ever saved.

Does it work in Edge, Brave or Opera?

Yes. It's a standard Manifest V3 extension, so any Chromium browser with MV3 support can load it.

Something broke. Where do I report it?

Open an issue on GitHub. The bug form asks for the video URL, your browser version and what the status chip said, which is usually enough to reproduce it.
